Negash

Description

Negash is a historic town in northern Ethiopia, located in the Tigray region, approximately 10 km east of the city of Wukro. It is an important religious site for Ethiopian Orthodox Christians, as it is believed to be the site where the first mosque in Ethiopia was established, as well as the site of the oldest Ethiopian Orthodox Church.
According to legend, the Prophet Muhammad sent some of his followers to Ethiopia in the early days of Islam to seek refuge from persecution. The followers were welcomed by the Christian ruler of Ethiopia, King Negus, who provided them with protection and allowed them to practice their religion freely. In gratitude, the followers built a mosque in Negashi, which is said to be the first mosque in Ethiopia and one of the oldest in Africa.
Today, Negashi is home to the Negashi St. Mary Ethiopian Orthodox Church, which is believed to be the oldest Ethiopian Orthodox Church and one of the oldest Christian churches in Africa. The church is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and attracts many pilgrims from around Ethiopia and beyond.
Negashi is also known for its beautiful scenery and ancient ruins, including the remains of the mosque built by the followers of the Prophet Muhammad. The mosque is now in ruins, but its importance as a historic site is recognized by the Ethiopian government and efforts are being made to preserve it.
In addition to its religious and historic significance, Negashi is also an important center for agriculture and commerce in the Tigray region. The town is surrounded by fertile farmland and is known for producing a variety of crops, including wheat, barley, teff, and sorghum.
