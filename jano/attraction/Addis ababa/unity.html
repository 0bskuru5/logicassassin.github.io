Unity Park

Description

PM Abiy Ahmed (Ph.D.) initiated the creation of a new boutique park within the Grand National Palace of Ethiopia. The construction and establishment of the park began in 2018 and officially opened to the public on October 10, 2019. The park offers visitors a wide range of activities and exhibits, including a black manned Ethiopian lion zoo, the banquet hall of Emperor Menelik II, the throne hall, and the ostrich and unity zoo, which houses Meerkats, an aquarium, peacocks, cheetahs, white lions, wild dogs, Gelada baboons, an aviary, and a safari. Additionally, the park features a pavilion dedicated to the various people and ethnic groups of Ethiopia, allowing visitors to learn about the country's rich history and cultural identity through electronic displays within the banquet hall.

Overall, Unity Park is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Ethiopia's rich history and culture, and for those who wish to experience the country's unique blend of tradition and modernity. It offers a rare opportunity to explore Ethiopia's past, present, and future in a beautiful and inspiring setting, and to connect with the country's vibrant and diverse communities.

What to Expect

Unity Park in Ethiopia is a magnificent public park that offers visitors a unique experience of history, culture, and natural beauty. Here are some of the things you can expect to see and do at Unity Park:

  1. Explore the Historical Buildings: Unity Park was once the palace of Emperor Haile Selassie I and has been renovated to showcase its historic architecture. Visitors can explore the palace's many rooms, including the emperor's office, bedroom, and reception hall. You can also see the crown jewels and other royal artifacts.
  2. Enjoy the Beautiful Gardens: The park is home to a variety of beautiful gardens, including the tropical garden, rose garden, and palm garden. You can stroll through the gardens and admire the colorful flowers, plants, and trees.
  3. Learn about Ethiopian Culture: Unity Park is also a cultural center that houses a museum showcasing the history and culture of Ethiopia. The exhibits include traditional clothing, handicrafts, and artifacts from various regions of Ethiopia. You can learn about the country's diverse ethnic groups and their cultural practices.
  4. Visit the Wildlife Conservation Center: Unity Park has a wildlife conservation center that features endangered species of animals such as the Ethiopian wolf and Gelada baboon. You can learn about the animals' conservation status and their natural habitat.
  5. Attend Public Events: Unity Park is also a venue for public events such as concerts, festivals, and exhibitions. You can check the park's calendar of events to see what's happening during your visit.

Overall, Unity Park is a great place to learn about Ethiopia's rich history, culture, and natural environment. It's an excellent destination for families, history buffs, and nature lovers alike.
