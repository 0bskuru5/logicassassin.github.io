Ethnography Museum

Description

The Ethnological Museum, located in a former imperial palace of Haile Selassie, offers a unique opportunity to explore the rich history and culture of Ethiopia. Visitors can discover the tradition of over 80 language groups represented in the museum's impressive collection of artifacts, which are thoughtfully arranged in period settings. This collection is widely regarded as one of the best in Africa and includes a variety of treasures such as Eastern Orthodox saints painted on wood, elaborate censers, illuminated medieval books, cowry shell jewelry, woodwork statues, carved stones, folk instruments, and exquisite neck rests. These items serve to showcase the full complexity of traditional life and culture in Ethiopia, offering visitors a deeper understanding of the country's layered history. Be sure not to miss the imperial bedroom and the diverse tribal heritage on display.
