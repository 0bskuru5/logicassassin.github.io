Tewodros II Square

Description

Tewodros II Square is dedicated to Ethiopia's first modern Emperor, Tewodros II, and his efforts to modernize the country's military and technology industry. After suffering a significant defeat against the technologically superior Egyptians, Tewodros II recognized the importance of modern armaments for Ethiopia's strength and unity. He established a technology industry village in Gafat and compelled imprisoned British citizens to manufacture a cannon, which they named Sebastopol. However, despite the significance that the Emperor placed on the cannon, it proved ineffective in battle. Nevertheless, Tewodros II's quest for technological advancement continues to inspire many Ethiopians. The square features a bronze replica of the original cannon as a tribute to the Emperor's vision and legacy.
