Debere Libanos Monastery and the Jemma River Valley

Description

Located 130 km from the Ethiopian capital of Addis Ababa, the 13th century AD Debere Libanos monastery offers a range of experiences for travelers. Along with its rich history, the area is known for its breathtaking scenery, diverse bird population, and hiking opportunities. The monastery, established by Ethiopian St. Tekelhyamnot, has been a center of monastic order and Ethiopian Orthodox Church education. Despite facing numerous challenges throughout its history, including destruction by the Italian army and a massacre that claimed the lives of 800 people, the modern church remains an important pilgrimage site, with its domed roof adorned with stained-glass windows. Visitors can also hike to the nearby Portuguese Bridge, which offers stunning views of the Jemma river valley. Though locals believe the stone-arch bridge was built by the Portuguese in the 16th century, it was actually constructed in the 1890s by Ras Darge in the Old Portuguese style. With its narrow span and impressive cascade below, the bridge makes for a spectacular photo opportunity.
